Origin

Built from what was lost, not what was imagined.

During Hurricane Helene in Asheville, the loss was not only physical. Home, car, personal belongings, and neighborhood stability were stripped away at once. But one of the deepest failures was communication itself.

When networks disappear, communities are pushed into isolation. Families cannot coordinate. Neighbors cannot organize. Information becomes fragmented right when it matters most.

NodeBlk began as a response to that reality: a device designed so communities are not left waiting on systems that can go dark.

Solution

A pocket-sized communication layer for when the grid is gone.

NodeBlk uses long-range radio to create local messaging without relying on cellular service or internet access. It is designed to operate during outages, blackouts, disasters, and unstable conditions.

The device combines three essentials: message exchange, community alerts, and offline survival guidance. That makes it useful not only in emergencies, but during the long aftermath when coordination and information matter just as much.

Direct messagingSend and receive text-based updates between nearby NodeBlk users.
Broadcast alertsShare neighborhood-wide messages about aid, resources, and urgent conditions.
Offline guidesStore local survival info, emergency steps, and community resource instructions.
Battery resilienceDesigned around low-power systems for extended use during outages.

Impact

Not just a device. Community infrastructure.

NodeBlk is designed to strengthen local resilience where conventional systems break down. It helps communities communicate faster, organize more clearly, and share critical information more directly.

This is especially important for under-resourced neighborhoods, shelters, mutual aid groups, churches, and families navigating uncertainty with limited support.

Pilot

Start with one neighborhood. Prove a new model.

The first NodeBlk pilot can focus on a small, high-need deployment with community partners. The goal is to test usability, messaging reliability, and practical value during preparedness drills or communication-disrupted conditions.

This framing makes NodeBlk not only a product, but a deployable resilience program funders can support.

Initial deployment50 devices distributed across one to two pilot communities.
