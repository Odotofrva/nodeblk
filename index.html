Origin

Built from what was experienced, not imagined.

This didn’t start as a product idea. It started with loss.

After Hurricane Helene, everything changed at once — housing, transportation, daily structure, and most importantly, communication. When networks go down, people don’t just lose signal — they lose coordination, access to information, and the ability to check on each other.

NodeBlk comes from that moment. It’s a response to what happens when people are left without a reliable way to connect.

Solution

A simple way to stay connected when the grid goes down.

NodeBlk is designed to work without relying on traditional infrastructure. No cell towers, no Wi-Fi, and no internet dependency.

It uses long-range communication to allow people nearby to send messages, share updates, and access important information. The goal isn’t complexity — it’s reliability when it matters most.

Direct messagingSend simple messages between nearby devices.
Broadcast alertsShare updates across a neighborhood in real time.
Offline guidanceAccess stored survival information without needing internet.
Battery-aware designBuilt to run efficiently during extended outages.

Pilot

Start small. Build something that actually works.

The first step is a focused pilot — placing devices into real communities and learning from how they’re actually used.

This isn’t about scaling too early. It’s about getting it right at the ground level, then growing from there with intention.

Initial deployment50 devices distributed across one to two pilot communities.
Core testingMessaging, alerts, guide access, and response coordination workflows.
Partner modelCommunity centers, shelters, churches, and local organizers as anchor nodes.
Funding usePrototype refinement, pilot builds, onboarding, and community feedback loops.

Phase 1

Finalize MVP hardware and interface for reliable field-ready messaging.

Phase 2

Deploy to a small group of users and partner sites for live testing.

Phase 3

Collect feedback, refine experience, and strengthen deployment playbooks.

Phase 4

Expand into a repeatable model for wider NYC and regional rollout.
