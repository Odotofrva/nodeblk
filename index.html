Why NodeBlk

Technology should serve the people who are helped last.

NodeBlk was shaped by the aftermath of Hurricane Helene in Asheville, North Carolina, where outages in cellular service, fuel access, and clean water exposed just how fragile daily systems can become.

It also exposed a deeper inequity: African American and underprivileged communities were often left waiting longer for support while tourist-centered areas recovered first. NodeBlk responds to that gap with a handheld device focused on resilience, access, and practical knowledge that stays available even when infrastructure does not.
